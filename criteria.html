Hey username, let me help you with that! Imagine this decision support system as a helpful friend, guiding you through the process of finding the perfect laptop for your informatics engineering studies.

You'll provide information on key criteria - screen size, weight, RAM, storage, and price. But the real magic happens when you assign a weight (very low, low, medium, high, very high) to each feature, telling the system your true preferences.

The system also looks at each criteria and assigns a "cost" or "benefit" ranking to them. If a particular feature is considered not-so-great, like a smaller screen or less storage, it gets a higher "cost" ranking, But if a feature is really desirable, like lots of RAM or a lightweight design, it earns a top "benefit".

Is a large screen a must-have, or do you prioritize portability? Do you need tons of storage for your engineering software? The system uses a method called Simple Additive Weighting to analyze all the options and score them based on your needs.

It's kind of like having a personalized checklist. The system takes into account both the laptop specifications and your weighted priorities, then presents you with a ranked list of the best options tailored to your unique requirements. Think of it as your own personal laptop matchmaker!
